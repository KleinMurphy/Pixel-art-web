Pixel Art

Pixel art is a form of computer graphics that uses "pixels" as the basic unit. Its characteristic is that the image is composed of pixels arranged in a matrix. When you zoom in to a certain extent, you can see the small particles inside, namely pixel particles. Pixel art, in the form of bitmap, has evolved from the most primitive computer image expression method to an independent digital art creation style, which is usually called "Pixel Art". The so-called pixel art does not refer to a single image, but emphasizes a style with clear outlines, bright colors, and an unconstrained style. This style is similar to the "pointillism" created by the French painter Seurat in the late 19th century. Pointillism is an oil painting method that uses small color dots to create an overall image. Painters of the pointillism school oppose the painting method of mixing colors on the drawing board. They only use four primary colors to paint, and use color dots to stack, just like the principle of television display, using the low resolution of the human retina to make people feel an overall image.
